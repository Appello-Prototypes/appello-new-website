# Progress Billing Automation: Getting Paid Faster

For ICI contractors working on large projects, progress billing is essential for cash flow. But traditional progress billing processes take 1-2 weeks—collecting field data, manually consolidating information, creating invoices, and entering them into accounting systems. Progress billing automation eliminates these delays, reducing billing cycles from weeks to days and improving cash flow significantly.

## The Progress Billing Challenge

### Traditional Process Delays

**Week 1: Data Collection**
- Field foremen submit timesheets
- Material purchases compiled
- Equipment usage tracked
- Subcontractor invoices collected
- Change orders documented

**Week 2: Consolidation and Billing**
- Office staff manually consolidates data
- Calculates percentage complete
- Creates progress invoice
- Enters into accounting system
- Sends to general contractor

**The Problem:**
- 10-14 day delay from work completion to invoice
- Manual data entry prone to errors
- Missing billable items
- Cash flow impact: $50,000+ monthly billing delayed 2 weeks = $1,370 in interest costs annually

## Progress Billing Methods Supported

### 1. Percentage Complete Method

**How It Works:**
- System tracks work completed vs. total contract
- Calculates percentage automatically
- Bills based on percentage × contract value
- Accounts for previous billings

**Example:**
- Contract: $500,000
- Work completed: 35%
- Previous billings: $100,000
- Current billing: ($500,000 × 35%) - $100,000 = $75,000

**Automation:**
- Labor hours tracked automatically
- Materials installed tracked automatically
- Percentage calculated automatically
- Invoice generated automatically

### 2. Milestone-Based Billing

**How It Works:**
- Contract divided into milestones
- Bill when milestone reached
- System tracks milestone completion
- Generates invoice automatically

**Example:**
- Milestone 1: Site prep (20%) = $100,000
- Milestone 2: Installation (40%) = $200,000
- Milestone 3: Completion (40%) = $200,000

**Automation:**
- Milestone completion tracked automatically
- Invoice generated when milestone reached
- Syncs to accounting automatically
- Payment tracking maintained

### 3. Time and Materials Billing

**How It Works:**
- Bill for actual labor hours × rates
- Bill for materials at cost + markup
- Bill for equipment usage
- System tracks all costs automatically

**Example:**
- Labor: 200 hours × $75/hour = $15,000
- Materials: $8,000 × 1.15 markup = $9,200
- Equipment: 40 hours × $50/hour = $2,000
- **Total: $26,200**

**Automation:**
- Timesheets sync automatically
- Material costs tracked automatically
- Equipment usage tracked automatically
- Markup applied automatically

This automation integrates with [real-time job costing](/blog/real-time-job-costing-benefits) systems to ensure progress billing reflects accurate, current project costs and profitability.
