# The Per Diem Calculation Challenge: How ICI Contractors Track Location-Based Allowances

ICI contractors provide per diem allowances to workers traveling for projects, but per diem rates vary dramatically by location. A worker traveling to New York City might earn $180 per day, while a worker traveling to rural Michigan earns $120 per day. Per diem rates change based on project location, duration, and company policy. Manual per diem tracking creates errors and administrative burden. Automated systems provide flexible per diem calculations with location-based rules, ensuring accurate reimbursement while reducing administrative overhead.

## The Per Diem Complexity Problem

Contractors provide per diem allowances to cover meals and incidental expenses for workers traveling away from home. Per diem rates vary by location based on cost of living differences. Urban areas like New York or Los Angeles require higher per diem rates than rural areas. Contractors must track per diem accurately to ensure workers are reimbursed fairly while controlling costs.

Manual per diem tracking requires office staff to look up rates for each location, calculate allowances based on work days, and process reimbursements. This process is time-consuming and error-prone, especially for contractors managing workers across multiple locations simultaneously.

## Why Per Diem Calculation is Challenging

### Location-Based Rate Variations

Per diem rates vary significantly by location. Federal per diem rates for 2025 range from $96 per day in low-cost areas to $309 per day in high-cost areas like New York City. Contractors using location-based per diem must track rates for each project location and apply them correctly.

An office manager calculating per diem for workers across 20 projects in different locations spends 6 hours per pay period looking up rates and calculating allowances. Each location has different rates that must be applied correctly. Manual lookup and calculation create opportunities for errors.

### Duration-Based Calculations

Per diem calculations depend on work duration, with different rates potentially applying to partial days. Workers traveling on Monday might earn full per diem, while workers returning on Friday might earn partial per diem. Duration tracking creates calculation complexity.

A contractor managing workers with varying travel schedules struggles to track work duration and apply correct per diem rates. Workers travel different days with different durations, creating tracking complexity. Manual calculation creates errors that affect reimbursement accuracy.

### Policy Variations

Different contractors have different per diem policies based on company standards and union agreements. Some contractors pay federal per diem rates, while others pay custom rates. Policy variations create calculation complexity when contractors work with multiple clients or union agreements.

An office manager calculating per diem under different policies spends 8 hours per pay period applying different rates and rules. Each policy has different rates and calculation methods. Manual application creates errors and inconsistencies.

### Integration with Travel Pay

Per diem often integrates with travel pay calculations, creating additional complexity. Workers earning travel pay might have different per diem rates or requirements. Integration of per diem and travel pay requires careful calculation to ensure accuracy.

An office manager calculating both travel pay and per diem spends 10 hours per pay period on combined calculations. Integration requires tracking both travel distances and work locations to apply correct rates. Manual calculation creates errors in both travel pay and per diem.
