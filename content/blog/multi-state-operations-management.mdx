# Multi-State Operations: Managing ICI Contractors Across Borders

For ICI contractors operating across multiple states, managing operations becomes exponentially more complex. Different states have different compliance requirements, travel pay rules vary by location, per diem rates differ, and certification tracking becomes critical. Multi-state operations require specialized tools and processes to maintain compliance and efficiency.

## Real-World Use Cases for ICI Contractors

### Use Case 1: Mechanical Insulation Contractor Manages 5-State Operations

A mechanical insulation contractor with 50 field workers operating across 5 states with multiple job sites and complex travel patterns struggled with manual tracking consuming 12 hours weekly, travel pay errors costing $3,000 monthly, and certification gaps occurring 2-3 times monthly. After implementing multi-state management systems with automated state-specific rules, tracking reduced to 2 hours weekly, travel pay errors decreased to $200 monthly, and certification gaps eliminated. The contractor saves $43,400 annually while maintaining compliance across all states.

### Use Case 2: HVAC Contractor Handles State-Specific Compliance

An HVAC contractor operating across 3 states with different licensing, safety regulations, and tax rules struggled with compliance challenges and manual tracking. After implementing multi-state management with state-specific rule configuration, all state requirements handled automatically. Compliance maintained across all states, manual tracking eliminated, and compliance risks reduced significantly.

### Use Case 3: Sheet Metal Contractor Manages Travel Pay Complexity

A sheet metal contractor with workers traveling between states struggled with travel pay calculations that varied by state and location.
