# The True Cost of Manual Systems for ICI Contractors

Many ICI contractors think manual systems are "free"—paper timesheets, Excel spreadsheets, text message scheduling. But manual systems have hidden costs that add up quickly: 15-20 hours per week of manual data entry, 5-15% revenue leakage from missed billable items, and 1-2 week billing delays. The true cost of manual systems is $25,000-$50,000 annually—often more than modern software costs.

## Revenue Leakage

### 1. Missed Change Orders

**The Problem:**
- Field workers forget to document change orders
- Change orders get lost
- Never make it to invoice
- **Revenue leakage: 5-15%**

**Example:**
- Monthly billing: $200,000
- Revenue leakage: 10% average
- **Lost revenue: $20,000/month = $240,000 annually**

### 2. Missed Billable Hours

**The Problem:**
- Overtime not tracked
- Travel time missed
- Equipment time not billed
- **Revenue leakage: 3-8%**

**Example:**
- Monthly billing: $200,000
- Revenue leakage: 5% average
- **Lost revenue: $10,000/month = $120,000 annually**

### 3. Billing Delays

**The Problem:**
- Manual consolidation takes 1-2 weeks
- Cash flow delayed
- Interest costs
- **Cost: $1,370-$2,740 annually**
