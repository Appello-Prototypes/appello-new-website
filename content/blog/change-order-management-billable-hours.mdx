# Change Order Management: Capturing Every Billable Hour

For ICI contractors, change orders are a fact of life. But when change orders aren't documented properly in the field, contractors lose 5-15% of their revenue—money that's rightfully theirs but never makes it to the invoice. According to industry studies, the average contractor loses $120,000-$360,000 annually to undocumented change orders. Proper change order management ensures every billable hour and material is captured, approved, and billed, transforming how contractors handle scope changes and protecting their rightful revenue.

### Revenue Leakage

**The Statistics:**

- Average contractor: $200,000 monthly billing

**Why It Happens:**

### Use Case 2: HVAC Contractor Captures Emergency Change Orders

An HVAC contractor working on commercial projects frequently encounters emergency change orders when building systems require immediate modifications. Previously, field workers would complete emergency work but forget to document it, resulting in lost billable hours. The contractor implemented mobile change order documentation that enables field workers to document change orders immediately from job sites, including photos and descriptions. Within 30 days, the contractor recovered $15,000 in previously lost emergency change order revenue, improving cash flow and ensuring all emergency work is properly billed.

### Use Case 3: Mechanical Insulation Contractor Streamlines Approval Process

A mechanical insulation contractor managing multiple projects simultaneously struggled with change order approval delays that delayed billing. Change orders would sit in email inboxes for days or weeks waiting for approval, delaying invoicing and cash flow. The contractor implemented an automated approval workflow with mobile notifications and status tracking. Change orders now move through approval in 2-3 days instead of 2-3 weeks, accelerating billing cycles and improving cash flow by $25,000 monthly.

### Use Case 4: Industrial Service Company Prevents Disputes

An industrial service company working on maintenance contracts frequently faced disputes over change order pricing and scope. Without proper documentation, general contractors would question change order validity, delaying payment and damaging relationships. The contractor implemented comprehensive change order documentation with photos, detailed descriptions, and approval tracking. Disputes decreased by 80%, payment cycles improved, and relationships strengthened through transparent documentation.

### Use Case 5: Multi-Trade Contractor Improves Change Order Tracking

A multi-trade contractor coordinating mechanical, electrical, and plumbing work across 20 active projects struggled to track change orders across multiple trades and projects. Change orders were scattered across email threads, spreadsheets, and paper forms, making it impossible to track status and ensure billing. The contractor implemented centralized change order management with project-based organization and automated status tracking. Change order visibility improved dramatically, billing accuracy increased, and the contractor recovered $180,000 annually in previously lost change order revenue.
